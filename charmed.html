The Artist

Tracy E. Meyer has been making handmade paper collages for over a decade, working out of her studio in Buffalo, New York. Every piece is built by hand from images cut out of vintage magazines, books, and other found paper — no digital tools, no reprints.

Her work draws on memory, music, nostalgia, and nature, arranging fragments of the past into images that feel both familiar and slightly surreal. Each collage is one of a kind, signed, mounted on watercolor paper, and ready for framing.

— Tracy

How Each Piece Is Made

Traditional cut-and-paste, start to finish

01

Gather

Old magazines, books, and paper ephemera are collected and sorted — color, texture, and subject all matter before a single cut is made.

02

Cut & Compose

Images are cut by hand and arranged on the page, shifted and reworked until unrelated fragments start to feel like they belong together.

03

Mount & Sign

The finished collage is glued to watercolor paper, signed, and finished — ready to frame, one of a kind, and never reprinted.
