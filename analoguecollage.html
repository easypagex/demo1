01 / THE ARTIST

Every piece starts the same way: a pile of secondhand magazines, a pair of scissors, and no undo button. Arte works entirely by hand — no digital layers, no Photoshop blending modes, just paper, glue, and an eye for images that argue with each other in interesting ways.

The result is a feed of #_analoguecollage work that feels tactile even on a screen: torn edges, visible glue lines, mismatched color eras colliding on one page. It's the kind of art you want to reach out and touch.

03 / HOW IT'S MADE

No layers. No undo.

01

Hunt for paper

Old magazines, flyers, book pages, and forgotten prints get rescued before the recycling bin — every scrap is a future ingredient.

02

Cut by hand

No lasso tool. Just scissors and a craft knife, cutting shapes freehand until two unrelated images start talking to each other.

03

Glue & commit

Once it's stuck, it's stuck. That permanence is the whole point — every piece carries the marks of the decision that made it.
