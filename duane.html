Paper, scissors, patience.

Duane works primarily in collage — cutting up old magazines, wrapping paper, index cards, and printed fragments, then rebuilding them into something new. Some pieces mix in digital elements; all of them start with his hands and a stack of scraps.

The result is a body of work that's textured, layered, and a little unpredictable — small compositions that reward a second look. He shares the process, the in-progress shots, and the finished pieces daily on Instagram.

What the work is about

Three threads, one practice

Identity

Who we're made of

Fragmented faces and figures built from mismatched paper — a reminder that identity is assembled, not fixed.

Impermanence

Nothing stays whole

Torn edges and layered scraps that embrace wear and change instead of hiding it.

Transformation

Old paper, new meaning

Discarded material — old magazines, wrapping paper — reborn as something worth a second look.
