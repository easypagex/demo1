The Artist

Paper, Scissors, Patience.

Lisa Pregent is a Cincinnati-based analog collage artist working the old-fashioned way — vintage magazines, hand-selected paper, a steady pair of scissors, and glue. No digital editing, no shortcuts. Just careful cutting and deliberate composition, one piece at a time.

She's a proud member of The International Collage Guild, part of a global community keeping the analog craft alive. Off the cutting mat, she's an unapologetic animal lover 🐶😻 and a dedicated gardener 🌸 — both of which quietly show up in her color palettes and subject matter.

How It's Made

Cut. Arrange. Paste. Repeat.

No filters, no layers panel — just paper. Here's what "analog collage" actually means in practice.

📚

Source

Vintage magazines, books, and ephemera are collected and sorted by color, texture, and subject.

✂️

Cut

Every shape is cut by hand with scissors or a blade — no scanners, no digital tracing.

🧩

Compose

Pieces are arranged and rearranged on the table until the composition finally clicks.

🖼️

Paste & Finish

The final layout is glued down, flattened, and prepped as an original or a print.
