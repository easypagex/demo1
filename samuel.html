About

Where design discipline meets the cut edge of paper

Samuel Eller works at the intersection of graphic design and fine art. Trained to postgraduate level and active as a design professor, he brings that same rigor to a personal practice built on collage, found imagery and visual poetry.

"A collage is never really finished — it's an argument between fragments that agree to sit still for a moment."

Each piece treats photographs, printed matter and typography as raw material: cut, reordered and reassembled until new meaning appears. The result sits comfortably in a gallery, a book cover, or a brand's visual identity.

"Teaching design and making collage are the same act, repeated at different speeds — both start by taking something apart to see how it works."
1

Gather

Source images, paper, type and texture become the working material — nothing starts from a blank page.

2

Cut & Sort

Fragments are separated from their original context and grouped by shape, colour and rhythm.

3

Compose

Pieces are layered and rearranged until an unplanned relationship starts to read as intentional.

4

Fix & Finish

The composition is locked in place — on paper, screen or print — ready to leave the studio.
