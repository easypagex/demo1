The manifesto

No blank page stays blank

Marko Köppe doesn't start with a sketch — he starts with a pile. Old magazines, receipts, wrapping paper, torn photographs, anything already lived-in and printed. The work begins by ripping that material apart, and it ends when the wreckage says something it never said before.

"Destroy and create" isn't a slogan here — it's the entire method. Nothing is precious until it's been cut.

How a piece gets made

Cut. Tear. Glue. Repeat.

Destroy

Gather & wreck

Magazines, packaging, old prints — anything printed and disposable gets collected, then torn or cut apart with no plan for how the pieces will be used.

Arrange

Fight for composition

Fragments get pushed around a surface for hours, sometimes days, until color, texture, and image start to argue with each other in the right way.

Create

Glue it down

Once the chaos finally agrees on something, it's fixed in place for good. What's left is a document of decisions that can never be undone.
