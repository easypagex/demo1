The artist

Torn paper, ink and instinct.

Sergio works across mediums the way a collage comes together — layer by layer, mixing what's printed with what's painted, what's cut with what's drawn. Graphic design training meets a grunge, DIY hand: rough edges, bold marks, and color that isn't afraid to clash.

Every piece starts analog — paper, blade, brush, screen — and some move on to digital finishing. Nothing here is a print run. What you see is what exists, and once it's sold, it's gone.

— Sergio

Six ways of making a mark

The disciplines

Graphic Design

Clean concepts built with the same instinct as the collage work — bold type, strong shapes, no filler.

Illustration

Character-driven pieces drawn by hand first, refined until the line feels inevitable.

Analog & Digital Collage

Cut paper, found imagery and scanned texture, assembled by hand and finished on screen.

Grunge Art

Distressed textures, spray and drip — a rougher visual language built on contrast.

Serigraphy

Hand-pulled screen prints — flat, punchy color layered the traditional way.

Acrylic Painting

Heavier, slower pieces — where the color and the canvas do the talking.
