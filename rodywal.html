the studio

Paper, glue,
and patience.

Rodywal is a one-person analog collage practice based in Leicestershire, UK. Every piece starts as a pile of found paper — old magazines, books, maps, packaging — and ends up as something none of them were on their own.

No digital tools touch the final image. What you see is what was physically cut, arranged, and stuck down by hand, which means every commission is genuinely one of one — it can't be recreated, resized, or reprinted the same way twice.

how it's made

Four steps. Zero undo button.

📚

Source

Old magazines, books, maps and ephemera are gathered and sorted by colour, texture and subject.

✂️

Cut

Every shape is cut by hand with scissors or scalpel — no digital tracing, no vector shortcuts.

🧩

Arrange

Fragments are laid out and re-laid out until the composition earns its place on the page.

🪡

Stick

Once it's right, it's glued down for good. What you get is the one and only original.
