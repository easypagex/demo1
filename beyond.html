The Explorer

Cacao & chocolate culture, from bean to bite.

Beyond the Bar started as one simple question: what actually happens between a cacao pod and a finished chocolate bar? Since then, Melanie has been learning, tasting, and creating alongside makers who care about where their cacao comes from — sharing it all with a growing community that wants their chocolate to mean something.

The Journey

From bean to bite

Every tasting walks through the real journey cacao takes before it reaches your tongue — so you'll never look at a chocolate bar the same way again.

01

Harvest

Ripe pods are cut by hand from the cacao tree, each one holding 30–40 beans.

02

Ferment

Beans rest in their pulp for days, developing the flavor precursors that make chocolate taste like chocolate.

03

Dry

Sun-dried slowly, locking in aroma before the beans ever leave the farm.

04

Roast

Careful roasting coaxes out the deep, complex notes hiding inside each bean.

05

Grind

Roasted nibs are ground into liquor, then conched into silky, glossy chocolate.

06

Taste

The moment it all comes together — and where every Beyond the Bar session begins.
