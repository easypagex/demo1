Our Story

Chocolate that starts with a handshake, not a factory line.

Shahamana Chocolate is the farm-to-bar arm of Shahamana Enterprise — a project built on the belief that great chocolate begins in the soil, not in a laboratory. Every bar we make can be traced back to the pod, the tree, and the farmer who tended it.

We work hand-in-hand with a cocoa farmers cooperative, keeping more of the value of chocolate with the people who grow it. And because we believe chocolate is better understood in person, we open our farm to visitors and volunteers year-round.

N Led by Nana Nyanteng Ahenkan, Founder
How It's Made

Five steps, one story: farm to bar

No shortcuts. Every bar of Shahamana chocolate passes through the same hands and the same hills.

1. Grow

Cooperative farmers cultivate cocoa in the shade of native trees.

2. Ferment

Fresh beans rest in wooden boxes, developing their signature flavor.

3. Dry

Beans are sun-dried on raised beds under the open Ghanaian sky.

4. Roast

Slow roasting unlocks the deep, nutty character of the bean.

5. Bar

Ground, conched and molded into the finished Shahamana bar.

The Cocoa Farmers Cooperative

Chocolate that puts farmers first

Shahamana Enterprise works directly with a cooperative of cocoa growers, building a chain that's shorter, fairer, and rooted in the community.

Grower Partnership

Cocoa is sourced directly from cooperative members at fair, transparent prices.

Shared Growth

Revenue from every bar helps reinvest in tools, training, and farm upkeep.

Quality at the Source

Cooperative training keeps fermentation and drying consistent, batch after batch.
