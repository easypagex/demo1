Where it grows

A traditional finca in Norte del Cauca

Our cacao grows on ancestral land in the north of Cauca, Colombia — a region shaped by generations of small farmers who never stopped tending cacao the traditional way. Every pod is watched, hand-picked at the right stage of ripeness, and cured slowly. It's cultivo y cultura: cultivation and culture, grown from the same soil.

  • 🌎 Grown on family-owned land in Colombia's cacao heartland, Norte del Cauca.
  • ✋ Hand-harvested pod by pod, selected only when fully ripe.
  • 📖 Traditional methods passed down through generations of growers.
  • 🤝 Direct relationships with buyers — no unnecessary middlemen.
From tree to bean

How we turn a pod into fine-flavor cacao

Every batch follows the same unhurried process — the one that gives Cacao de Rama its character.

01

Harvest

Pods are cut by hand only once they reach full ripeness on the tree.

02

Ferment

Beans rest and ferment in wooden boxes, developing the aroma that defines the finca's flavor.

03

Dry

Sun-dried slowly and turned by hand until moisture and flavor are perfectly balanced.

04

Select

Every batch is sorted by hand before it leaves the finca, ready for your kitchen or workshop.
