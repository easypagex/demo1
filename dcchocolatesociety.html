Our Story

Started by three friends who never missed a festival.

Three chocolate lovers who attended every DC Chocolate Festival since 2016 wanted the feeling of that community year-round. In early 2019, they started hosting casual monthly meetings — and DC Chocolate Society was born. Harper Macaw hosted the very first one.

Who it's for

Anyone who wants to eat chocolate, learn a little, and enjoy the company of other chocolate lovers — no tasting resume needed. Attend one meeting or every meeting, it's entirely up to you.

How It Works

Simple, casual, and always about the chocolate

Every meeting follows the same relaxed rhythm — RSVP, taste, talk.

📝

RSVP on Eventbrite

Reserve your spot so we know how much chocolate to buy and how much space to set up. That's the only "rule."

🍫

Taste with a guest maker

Each meeting features a different bean-to-bar maker or single-origin flight, with the story behind the chocolate.

💬

Talk and connect

Sit back, relax, and enjoy the company of other chocolate lovers — or just enjoy the chocolate. Either way works.

Good to Know

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to know anything about chocolate?

No, absolutely not! This is a casual gathering for anyone who wants to eat chocolate and maybe learn a bit along the way — not to become an official taster.

Do I have to come to every meeting?

Not at all. Attend as many or as few meetings as you wish, whenever it fits your schedule.

Why do I need to RSVP?

RSVPs help us plan how much chocolate to buy and how much space to set up, so everyone has enough to taste.

Where do I find upcoming dates and tickets?

Everything — Eventbrite listings, Instagram, and more — is linked from our Linktree.
