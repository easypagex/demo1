About the artist

Language, memory, and the paper trail we leave behind.

Torea builds each piece from what already existed — someone else's magazine, someone else's letter, someone else's discarded photograph — and gives it a second life.

Torea Frey is a Portland-area collage and mixed-media artist creating contemporary work from found materials, exploring language, identity, and memory. Her pieces are built by hand from vintage magazines, books, and mail-related ephemera, often finished with graphite and ink.

She's known for daily and durational practices — pieces made card by card, day by day — and for curatorial work that brings other artists' voices into the room alongside her own.

Analog collage Mixed media Mail art Disability arts

Where the work has shown up: a solo exhibition of collage on paper at Float On in Southeast Portland; a cover commission for a print anthology of collage and mixed-media work; ongoing series shared card-by-card with a growing following.

How she works: nothing is generated or printed first — every scrap is cut, torn, or salvaged, then composed by hand. The result reads less like illustration and more like evidence: layered, textured, a little frayed at the edges on purpose.

New collectors most often start the same way — by scrolling the feed, then sending a DM.

Ongoing series & bodies of work

Every series lives first on Instagram, where new pieces post as they're finished. This is the shorthand — follow along for the full archive.

Series 01

Defaced Women

Mixed-media works on paper grappling with gender and identity, exhibited as a solo show at Float On in Southeast Portland.

Series 02

Index Card a Day

A durational, one-a-day collage project — small-format pieces made rapid-fire from found paper, one index card at a time.

Series 03

One Thing Leads to Another

A 100-day generative collage project: the first piece sparked the next, and the next, until 100 works existed as one continuous chain. Now a printed book.

Series 04

Let 'Er Rip

A curated group exhibition of collage and disability arts, built from a shared source of paper and bringing two dozen Pacific Northwest artists into one show.

The book

A hundred days, one continuous thread.

This art book documents a 100-day analog collage project in which the very first piece became the springboard for the next 99 — each one answering, echoing, or contradicting the one before it. Every finished work is reproduced in full color, alongside commentary on the process behind it.

Curatorial work

Let 'Er Rip: collage and disability arts

Torea curated Let 'Er Rip, a community collage exhibition inspired by the work of nonverbal paper artist Neva Delashmutt Sincick — pairing her own new collage pieces with fresh work from two dozen Pacific Northwest artists, all made from a shared source of paper. On view at 811 SE Stark Street, Portland, January through July 2025.
