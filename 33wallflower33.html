Artist statement

Old photographs, new walls.

"A visual ode to the past" — figures borrowed from another century, left to weather in this one.

33wallflower33 works in the wheatpaste tradition — printed paper, brushed with paste, pressed onto brick, plywood, and painted-over storefronts across Toronto. The imagery leans sepia and monochrome: women and children with the stillness of an old family photograph, often carrying a short, quiet line of text.

The work has appeared alongside the city's most recognizable street art, from the laneways of Graffiti Alley to group installations documented by local street art archives — pieces that don't ask for attention so much as reward a second look.
