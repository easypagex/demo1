The practice

Every piece starts as loose paper on a table.

Old magazines, found ephemera, torn edges, glue that never quite dries flat — Martín works analog collage layer over layer, entirely by hand. There's no undo, no digital shortcut, just scissors, blade, and paper doing exactly what paper does. It's slow, deliberate work, and that's the point: you can see the hand in every piece.

Alongside the paper practice runs a second creative life on screen: digital collage published under the name soyazulmartin, exploring the same instincts for cutting, pairing, and recomposing images — just with a different set of tools.

— Martín

A second voice, built pixel by pixel instead of paper by paper. Same eye for juxtaposition and composition, translated into a parallel digital practice under @soyazulmartin.
