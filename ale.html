About Ale

An artist working in paper and thread

Ale De Niebla is a Valdivia-based artist working at the intersection of collage and textile artivism. Her practice moves between cutting, layering, and sewing — treating found paper and fabric as material for both personal expression and community education.

Alongside her own work, Ale teaches and organizes through Taller Collage, and is an active part of Valdivia's textile and artivist scene — collaborating with local circles, cultural spaces, and coastal collectives that use craft as a tool for education and social practice.

The Practice

Three ways Ale works

Her practice sits between personal art-making and community teaching — each part feeding the others.

01 / Collage

Collage art ✂️

Cut-paper compositions built from layering, texture, and found imagery — original pieces shaped by hand, one cut at a time.

02 / Textile

Artivismo & Textile Education

Workshops and projects that use textile making as a form of artivism — connecting craft, community, and social practice around Valdivia.

03 / Taller

Taller Collage

Hands-on collage sessions where participants learn technique while building their own work — open to beginners and returning makers alike.
