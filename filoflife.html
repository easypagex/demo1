The curator

An eye trained on quiet moments

Catherine Le Hénan founded FilofLife as a place to keep the images that stopped her — a self-portrait in Avignon light, a stranger's silhouette on the Seine, a still from a film that never left her. What began as a personal archive is now followed by a community of 214,000 people who return for the same reason she started it.

"Every frame is a small argument that beauty is still worth stopping for."

FilofLife sits between eras on purpose — pairing found vintage photography with contemporary work, so the feed reads less like a gallery and more like a memory you half-recognise. Based in Paris, curated everywhere.
