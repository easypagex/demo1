The Artist

Self-taught. Toronto-based. Obsessed with old paper.

Robyn Redish has been hand-cutting collage since she was a kid, armed with scissors and whatever magazine was lying around. That habit never really stopped — it just got sharper.

Every piece starts as a stack of decades-old pages before it becomes something that never existed.

Today she works exclusively in analog: vintage National Geographic, LIFE Magazine, and old newspapers collected from across Toronto, hand-selected for color, texture, and the strange little details that only show up in print from another era. Each collage is sliced, layered, and assembled by hand — no digital compositing, ever.

Her work has shown in galleries, sold to collectors internationally, and been featured in print publications covering the collage art world.

How it's made

Paper in. Something stranger out.

01 / SOURCE

Vintage pages

Old National Geographic, LIFE, and newspaper issues are collected from around Toronto — flea markets, estate sales, secondhand shops — and sorted by color, texture, and subject.

02 / CUT

Hand-sliced

Every element is cut by hand with a scalpel — no digital scissors, no shortcuts. Figures, textures, and landscapes are freed from their original pages one careful pass at a time.

03 / BUILD

Layered & glued

Fragments are arranged, tested, and re-arranged until the composition clicks — then permanently layered and adhered to become a one-of-a-kind, physical original.
