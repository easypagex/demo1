Self-taught · Making since the 2020 lockdown

Paper scraps, second-hand finds, and a lot of glue.

Kim picked up her first collage during lockdown and never put it down. She's a self-taught mixed media artist working out of Springfield, Missouri — and a committed thrift store junkie who treats every trip to the dollar store like a supply run.

Her work leans into texture and experimentation: torn book pages, gelli-printed papers, stencils, medical tape repurposed as washi, and whatever else survives the cutting mat. She posts the process, not just the result — the false starts included.
