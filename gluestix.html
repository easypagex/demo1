The artist

Paper doesn't lie — it just gets rearranged.

J. Møller builds every scene by hand: no tablets, no layers panel, no undo button. Just salvaged magazines, old photographs, and a blade that only gets one shot at each cut.

Each piece starts as a pile of scraps and ends as a small, self-contained world — a reminder that looking closer is always worth it.

"I don't make images disappear into the page. I make you look twice — that's the whole trick."
— J. Møller, gluestix.art

How a gluestix piece gets made

Four steps. Zero shortcuts.

Source

Old magazines, books, and photographs are collected and sorted by color, texture, and era — the raw material for everything that follows.

Cut

Every shape is hand-scissored, chosen as much for its scale as its subject — no two edges are ever quite the same.

Arrange

Pieces are laid out, rearranged, and photographed dozens of times before a single one is glued down for good.

Glue

Layers are fixed in reverse order with a trusty glue stick, then sealed — a finished world, built entirely by hand.
